"The first 90 minutes are the most important "

I have always enjoyed all types of sports, but soccer was my favourite. I started playing that game since I was very young. It all started when my neighbor and I bought our first ball and started playing on the street next to the building we lived in. Then I decided to take the next step by joining an academy. At that time, I was already in a basketball academy, but I have decided to quit since it was clear to me that I enjoy soccer a lot more. From that moment it became my obsession. The only places to find me if I am not at practice nor school, would be on the street playing more soccer, or in my bedroom watching soccer videos.

Soon I started improving a lot and at that time, I was seen as the team's leader especially when we started competing in tournaments. As the years went by, I kept improving until I became the team's captain and I got drafted to the National Soccer Team, at the age of 14. Unfortunately, about a year and half later I decided to give up on my dream of becoming a professional player and focus on my education. I stepped back a little bit and gave it less time but I kept playing frequently: at least 5 hours a week.

When I got into college, I started practicing again with my old team, but I knew that it was going to be no more than a hobby of mine. A year later, I transferred to William Paterson University and joined there men's soccer team for one year before transeferring again to NJIT.
